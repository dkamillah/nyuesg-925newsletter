September Greetings!

Welcome back, teacher residents! September is here, and with it comes that unmistakable buzz of new beginnings – fresh notebooks, newly decorated classrooms, and the exciting energy of students settling into their routines. This month marks a special milestone in your residency journey as you step into your mentor's classroom, becoming keen observers and eager learners. While your students are getting to know their new grade level, you're diving deep into the art of teaching by watching master educators work their magic during those crucial first weeks. Soak it all in – from the way your mentor greets students at the door to how they seamlessly weave classroom expectations into daily activities. These September observations will become the foundation for your own teaching practice, so grab your notebooks, keep your eyes wide open, and get ready for an incredible month of learning alongside some of the best teachers in the field!

📋 September Certification Checklist

Staying on top of your certification timeline is crucial to ensuring you complete all requirements by your June 2026 deadline – your future teaching career depends on meeting these milestones, and your Residency Directors will be conducting regular check-ins to help keep you on track for success.

🌟 Kudos to Yasmeen Beadle, who exemplified this proactive approach by using her summer wisely to complete credit deficiencies and tackle not one, but TWO certification exams before the school year even began – what an inspiring way to set yourself up for success! 🌟

NYU Teacher Residency Timeline: September

Observe and participate in planning meetings Attend all planning sessions for the section(s) they will eventually teach to understand curriculum development and lesson preparation processes.
Watch and learn from experienced teachers Focus on observing how their teaching mentor establishes classroom routines, builds relationships with students, and sets expectations during the critical first days of school.
Take detailed notes on teaching practices Document observations about classroom management techniques, instructional strategies, and student engagement methods used by their mentor teacher.
Work with individual students Provide one-on-one support and assistance to students when appropriate, gaining hands-on experience with student interactions.
Facilitate small group activities Lead or assist with small group work to practice instructional skills in a more manageable setting before taking on whole-class responsibilities.
Avoid whole-class instruction Resist the urge to jump into full teaching mode and instead focus on the learning and observation phase that's crucial for their development.
Study classroom setup and environment Observe how the physical classroom space is organized and how the learning environment is established to support student success.
